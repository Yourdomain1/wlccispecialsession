IEEE WCCI 2026 Special Session

Advances in Trustworthy XAI: Methodologies, Benchmarking & Diverse Data Contexts

Organizers & Contact

Organizers: Pr. Imen Jdey (REGIM Lab, Tunisia), Pr. M. Tanveer (IIT Indore, India), Pr. Fadoua Drira (REGIM Lab, Tunisia), Pr. Rahma Fourati (REGIM Lab, Tunisia)

Primary Contact: imen.jdey@ieee.org

Aim and scope

This special session addresses the crucial need for methodological rigor and standardized benchmarking in eXplainable AI (XAI) across diverse data modalities (image, audio, tabular, multimodal). We invite research that develops, compares, and rigorously evaluates novel XAI techniques to strengthen transparency and trust in computational intelligence systems. The session creates a dedicated venue for this rapidly growing field, attracting high-quality submissions and fostering new collaborations. WCCI provides an ideal forum for this discussion, as it is the world’s premier event in computational intelligence, resulting from the convergence of IJCNN, IEEE CEC, and FUZZ-IEEE.

Special Activity: Open Discussion Slot (ODS)

To enhance engagement, the session will incorporate an Open Discussion Slot (ODS), following the paper presentations. This interactive forum allows attendees to share insights, raise critical questions, and collectively brainstorm solutions. The ODS is designed to bridge the gap between theoretical research and practical challenges, encouraging new collaborations and identifying future research directions in XAI.

Topics of Interest

  • Explainable and Transparent AI (XAI) for Image and Audio Analysis
  • Interpretable Deep Learning Models for Array and Tabular Data
  • Multimodal Explainability: Fusing Explanations Across Different Data Types
  • Novel Visualization Techniques for Model Interpretations
  • Evaluating the Human-in-the-Loop Aspects of XAI
  • Algorithmic Audits and Trustworthiness of AI Systems
  • Mitigating Bias in Explanations and Ensuring Fairness
  • Causality and Counterfactual Explanations for Different Data Modalities
  • Applying XAI to Fuzzy Systems and Evolutionary Computation
  • Benchmark Datasets and Metrics for Evaluating Explanations
