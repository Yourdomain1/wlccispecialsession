Advances in Trustworthy XAI: Methodologies, Benchmarking & Diverse Data Contexts

Abstract

This special session aims to advance methodological rigor and benchmarking in eXplainable AI (XAI) across diverse data modalities—image, audio, tabular, and multimodal contexts. It invites research that develops, compares, and evaluates novel XAI techniques to strengthen transparency and trust in computational intelligence.

  • Novel XAI methods tailored to different data modalities
  • Comparative studies and benchmarking of interpretable models
  • Standardized evaluation metrics and protocols for explanations

Topics of Interest

  • XAI methodologies for image, audio, and time-series data
  • Comparative benchmarking of interpretable models
  • Fusion and evaluation of multimodal explanations
  • Metrics for human-in-the-loop explainability
  • Auditing and bias quantification in explanations
  • Causal and counterfactual explainability
  • XAI for fuzzy and evolutionary systems
  • Benchmark datasets and evaluation protocols
