Advances in Trustworthy XAI: Methodologies, Benchmarking & Diverse Data Contexts

Aim and Scope

This special session addresses the crucial need for methodological rigor and standardized benchmarking in Explainable AI (XAI) across diverse data modalities (image, audio, tabular, multimodal). We invite research that develops, compares, and rigorously evaluates novel XAI techniques to strengthen transparency and trust in computational intelligence systems.

WCCI provides an ideal venue for this discussion, as it is the world’s premier event in computational intelligence, resulting from the convergence of IJCNN, IEEE CEC, and FUZZ-IEEE.

Special Activity: Open Discussion Slot (ODS)

To enhance engagement, the session will include an Open Discussion Slot (ODS) following the presentations. This interactive forum encourages attendees to raise questions, share insights, and collaboratively identify future research directions in XAI.

Topics of Interest

  • Explainable and Transparent AI (XAI) for Image and Audio Analysis
  • Interpretable Deep Learning for Tabular and Array Data
  • Multimodal Explainability
  • Novel Visualization Techniques
  • Human-in-the-loop Evaluation for XAI
  • Algorithmic Audits and Trustworthy AI
  • Bias Mitigation in Explanations
  • Causality and Counterfactuals
  • XAI in Fuzzy Systems and Evolutionary Computation
  • Benchmarking and Metrics for Evaluations
