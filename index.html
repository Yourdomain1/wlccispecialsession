Abstract

The widespread adoption of computational intelligence across various high-stakes domains, from computer vision to signal processing and beyond, is heavily reliant on our ability to understand and trust the decisions made by these complex models. To ensure responsible deployment, this trust must be built upon **rigorous methodological frameworks and clear, standardized evaluation protocols. This special session will serve as a focused, interdisciplinary forum to explore novel methodological frameworks, concrete techniques, and rigorous benchmarking in eXplainable and interpretable AI (XAI). Our specific emphasis will be on methodological innovation for diverse data modalities: images, audio (signal), array data, and multimodal data, coupled with clear evaluation protocols. We will welcome contributions that tackle fundamental challenges by focusing on:
  • Developing and detailing novel XAI methodologies tailored to different data types.
  • Comparative studies and rigorous benchmarking of existing and new XAI techniques.
  • Establishing standardized metrics and protocols for evaluating explanations across diverse real-world applications.
The ultimate goal is to foster a dialogue that moves beyond pure performance metrics to a holistic framework for responsible and transparent AI deployment.

Relevance to WCCI

This special session aligns directly with the themes of the WCCI's three flagship conferences:

IJCNN (International Joint Conference on Neural Networks)

The session will showcase the latest advancements in XAI for deep neural networks, including CNNs, ViTs, and other architectures, which are central to modern computer vision and signal processing.

FUZZ-IEEE (IEEE International Conference on Fuzzy Systems)

We will explore how fuzzy logic and fuzzy systems can be used to develop intrinsically interpretable models and to provide human-understandable explanations for black-box models.

IEEE CEC (IEEE Congress on Evolutionary Computation)

The session will address the application of evolutionary algorithms to optimize and generate more interpretable models, as well as the use of XAI to understand the decision-making process of complex evolutionary systems.

Additional Activities

Open Discussion Slot (ODS)

To enhance participant engagement and foster deeper dialogue, this special session will incorporate an interactive discussion format designed to go beyond traditional paper presentations and create a collaborative environment for attendees.

Format

Facilitated, interactive discussion following paper presentations

Duration

30-minute dedicated slot at session end

Facilitators

Session organizers M. Tanveer and Imen Jdey will co-facilitate the discussion.

Purpose

Bridge theory and practice, encourage collaborations, and identify future research directions in XAI.

Expected Benefits

The ODS will serve as a dynamic forum for attendees to share insights, raise critical questions, and brainstorm solutions collectively. It will allow for a free-flowing exchange of ideas that may not fit into the Q&A segment of individual paper presentations. This format is intended to bridge the gap between theoretical research and practical challenges, encouraging new collaborations and helping to identify future research directions in XAI for diverse data modalities.
