Abstract and Rationale

This special session addresses the crucial need for **methodological rigor and standardized benchmarking in eXplainable AI (XAI)** across diverse data modalities, including image, audio, tabular, and multimodal contexts. We invite research that develops, compares, and rigorously evaluates novel XAI techniques to strengthen **transparency and trust** in computational intelligence systems.

The session will create a dedicated venue for this rapidly growing and critical field, attracting high-quality submissions and fostering new collaborations. **WCCI** provides an ideal forum for discussion of these issues, given that it is the largest technical event in the field of Computational Intelligence (CI) at large, resulting from the convergence of **IJCNN, FUZZ-IEEE, and CEC.**

Special Activity: Open Discussion Slot (ODS)

The special session will further enhance participant engagement and foster deeper dialogue through an **Open Discussion Slot (ODS)**. This will be a facilitated, interactive **30-minute discussion** led by the session chairs, immediately following all scheduled paper presentations. The ODS is designed as a dynamic forum for attendees to share insights, raise critical questions, and collectively **brainstorm solutions**. This format aims to bridge the gap between theoretical research and practical challenges, encouraging new collaborations and helping to **identify future research directions** in XAI for diverse data modalities.

Topics of Interest

  • Novel XAI methodologies for image, audio, and time-series data
  • Comparative studies and **benchmarking** of interpretable models
  • Metrics for **human-in-the-loop** explainability
  • Fusion and evaluation of multimodal explanations
  • Auditing and **bias quantification** in explanations
  • Causal and counterfactual explainability techniques
  • XAI for **fuzzy and evolutionary systems**
  • Benchmark datasets and standardized evaluation protocols
