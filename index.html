Ethical and Explainable Computational Intelligence in Medical and Health Systems

A Special Session at the IEEE World Congress on Computational Intelligence (WCCI) 2026

Abstract

The widespread adoption of computational intelligence systems, including neural networks, fuzzy systems, and advanced learning algorithms, in critical healthcare applications necessitates a rigorous focus on their responsible and trustworthy development. This special session will serve as an interdisciplinary forum to explore the challenges and opportunities at the intersection of computational intelligence and healthcare systems. Building on the core expertise of the organizers in deep learning for medical image analysis, privacy-preserving techniques, robust fuzzy systems, wearable devices, and AI ethics, this session will bridge foundational research with its real-world ethical, legal, and social implications.

We will welcome contributions that address how to build robust and fair AI systems for clinical diagnostics, ensure data privacy through federated and blockchain-based learning, enhance model interpretability, and design intelligent wearable/implantable devices. The session's topics are directly relevant to IJCNN, FUZZ-IEEE, and IEEE CEC, as they involve fundamental challenges in algorithmic fairness, explainability, and the application of advanced computational paradigms to sensitive data. The potential impact is to foster collaborations among AI researchers, medical professionals, and ethicists, accelerating the development of a new generation of healthcare AI that is not only effective but also ethically sound and transparent.

Topics of Interest

We invite submissions on a wide range of topics related to computational intelligence and its responsible application in healthcare, including but not limited to:

  • Ethical and Algorithmic Audits of AI Systems for Clinical Decision Support
  • Mitigating Bias and Ensuring Fairness in Healthcare AI
  • Explainable and Transparent AI for Clinical Decision-Making
  • Privacy-Preserving Techniques (Federated Learning, Blockchain) in Medical Data Analysis
  • Intelligent Wearable and Implantable Devices for Automated Diagnosis and Monitoring
  • Multiscale Modeling of Biological Systems using Computational Intelligence
  • Legal and Regulatory Frameworks for Medical AI
  • AI for Public Health, Epidemiology, and Resource Allocation
  • Human-in-the-Loop Systems and Shared Decision-Making in Clinical Practice
  • The Role of Computational Intelligence in Bioinformatics and Genomics

Committed Papers (Highly Recommended)

The following papers are already committed to being submitted to this special session:

  • Title: A Blockchain-Based Federated Learning Mechanism for Privacy-Preserving Breast Cancer Diagnosis
    Authors: Imen Jdey, Wided Moulahi, Tarek Moulahi, ...

    This paper proposes a novel framework that combines federated learning and blockchain to enable secure and privacy-preserving breast cancer diagnosis across distributed hospital networks, directly building on recent work by the session organizers.

  • Title: A Fuzzy-Based Support Vector Machine for Accurate Alzheimer's Disease Classification from MRI
    Authors: M. Tanveer, [Co-authors]

    We introduce a new fuzzy support vector machine (FSVM) model designed to improve the classification accuracy of Alzheimer's disease by effectively handling noisy and class-imbalanced neuroimaging data, a key challenge in the field.

  • Title: A Framework for AI Trustworthiness Assessment in Medical Wearable Devices
    Authors: Dimitrios I. Fotiadis, [Co-authors]

    This paper presents a framework for assessing the trustworthiness of AI systems integrated into wearable medical devices, focusing on key metrics like accuracy, robustness, and the human-machine interface from the perspective of both patients and clinicians.

  • Title: Ethical and Algorithmic Audits of AI Systems for Clinical Decision Support
    Authors: Drock Brand, [Co-authors]

    This paper explores a methodology for conducting ethical and algorithmic audits on AI models used in healthcare. It focuses on identifying and mitigating biases related to race, gender, and socioeconomic status to ensure equitable patient outcomes.

  • Title: Towards Secure Medical Image Sharing Using Watermarking and Computational Intelligence
    Authors: Fadoua Drira, [Co-authors]

    This work proposes a novel watermarking technique for securing medical images, combining robust embedding methods with intelligent algorithms to ensure data integrity and confidentiality during transfer, a critical need for remote diagnostics.
